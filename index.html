Introduction

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, the esteemed Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, entered this world on October 15, 1931. Renowned for his pivotal contributions to missile and nuclear weapon projects, he earned the well-deserved moniker "The Missile Man." Despite his remarkable achievements, Kalam maintained a remarkably humble and unassuming lifestyle. He possessed a deep fondness for literature and even composed poetry. Remarkably, he never married, and he was known for personally addressing his failures while attributing success to others. Dr. Kalam remained active and dedicated until his very last moments, passing away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM).

Timeline

  • 1931 - 1950s:
    • October 15, 1931: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, to a humble family.
    • Growing up in a modest environment, young Kalam showed an early aptitude for science and an insatiable curiosity.
  • 1954: Graduates from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics.
  • 1955 - 1960s: Pursues a degree in aeronautical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960: Joins the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE), a branch of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1969: Transitions to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) to work under the leadership of Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, contributing to the development of satellite launch vehicle technology.
  • 1980: Appointed as the Project Director for India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III. Under his leadership, India successfully launches the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.
  • 1982: Receives the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards, for his contributions to the country's space and defense programs.
  • 1992: Becomes the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1997: Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, for his pioneering work in space and military technology.
  • 1998: Plays a pivotal role in India's successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests, establishing the country as a nuclear weapons state.
  • 2002 - 2007:
    • Serves as the 11th President of India, succeeding Dr. K. R. Narayanan.
    • During his presidency, Dr. Kalam emphasizes education, particularly the importance of inspiring and guiding the youth, often interacting with students and encouraging them to dream big.
  • 2005: Proposes the concept of "PURA" (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas), which advocates for rural development and poverty eradication through the integration of modern amenities in rural regions.
  • 2015: On July 27, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passes away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving the world in mourning.

Books

The President, beloved by the people, possessed a remarkable gift for authoring books, earning him even greater admiration and inspiration. Some of his notable works include:
  • "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium."
  • "Wings of Fire: An Autobiography."
  • "Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Potential of India."
  • "The Luminous Sparks: A Poetic and Colorful Biography."
  • "Guiding Souls: Conversations on Life's Meaning."
  • "Mission of India: A Vision for the Youth of India."

Legacy

Dr. Kalam's inspirational books, "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," continue to motivate generations of young Indians and others worldwide. His commitment to education and interaction with students inspire countless young minds to pursue their dreams, fostering a love for science and technology. His vision for a developed India remains a guiding force in the nation's progress, particularly in the fields of science, technology, and innovation. The Kalam Foundation and various institutions and initiatives established in his honor continue to carry forward his work and legacy. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's life was a testament to the power of dreams, dedication, and determination. Despite humble beginnings, he rose to become one of India's most celebrated scientists and leaders, leaving a lasting impact on the fields of science, technology, and education. His legacy continues to inspire and shape the future of India and the world.
